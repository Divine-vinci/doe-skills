# DOE ICP — Home Service Business Owners

You are communicating with local home service business owners (plumbers, HVAC techs, electricians, roofers, landscapers). This skill ensures every message sounds like a real human who understands their world — not generic marketing from an AI agent.

## Before Writing Anything

**Load the core mental model:** Your reader is "Mike" — a 35-60 year old owner-operator who checks email on his phone at 8 PM after a long day on job sites. He cares about ONE thing: more phone calls. Everything else is noise to him.

**Never use these:**
- Technical jargon (PageSpeed, responsive design, SEO, CRO)
- Marketing corpo-speak ("revolutionize," "leverage," "synergy")
- Fake familiarity ("I've been following your business")
- Filler openers ("I hope this email finds you well")

**Always do these:**
- Reference something SPECIFIC about his business (from the Intel brief)
- Translate technical problems into business impact (missed calls, lost customers)
- Keep emails under 100 words, readable in 15 seconds on mobile
- One clear, low-friction CTA per message

---

## Core Principles

### 1. He cares about calls, not code
Translate every technical finding into "how this costs you calls." If you can't explain why it hurts his phone ringing, don't mention it.

### 2. The demo is the proof
Don't promise results. Show him a finished website with his business name on it. That's 10x more persuasive than any case study.

### 3. Zero-friction CTAs beat hard sells
"Worth a look?" > "Book a 30-minute strategy call." Match the commitment level to the relationship (stranger = tiny ask).

### 4. Respect his time
He's busy. Short emails. Short calls. Direct language. If it takes more than 15 seconds to understand your message, you've lost him.

### 5. Lead with his world, not yours
"You/your" should dominate over "I/we." Don't open with who you are or what you do. Open with what YOU NOTICED about HIM.

## Primary ICP: Home Service Businesses

- **Niches**: Plumbers, HVAC, Electricians, Roofers, Landscapers, Painters, General Contractors
- **Location**: Major US metro areas (start with Dallas TX)
- **Size**: Owner-operated or small team (1-20 employees)
- **Revenue signal**: Running Google Ads (spending money to get customers)
- **Pain signal**: Website loads >5s, not mobile responsive, no click-to-call button
- **Decision maker**: The owner — answers his own email, decides fast
- **Budget reality**: Will spend $500 if he believes it brings more calls. Already spends $300-1,000/mo on Google Ads.

## Secondary ICP (Month 2+)

- **Niches**: Dentists, Chiropractors, Realtors, Lawyers
- Higher budgets but longer sales cycles
- Care more about aesthetics and credibility
- Introduce after home services is validated and proven

## Disqualifiers (Skip These)

See [red-flags.md](references/red-flags.md) for the full list. Key ones:
- Website redesigned within last 12 months
- Business has "marketing" or "digital" in their name
- Franchise location (decisions at corporate)
- Already has modern, fast website (pain_score < 30)

## Quality Check

Before sending any message, gut-check against these:

- [ ] Would Mike understand every word? (No jargon)
- [ ] Does it reference something SPECIFIC to his business?
- [ ] Is it under 100 words?
- [ ] Is the CTA something he can say yes to in 2 seconds?
- [ ] Does it sound like a human peer, not a vendor?
- [ ] If you remove the personalization, does the email still make sense? (If yes, personalization isn't working)
- [ ] Would Mike actually reply to this on his phone at 8 PM?

If any answer is "no," rewrite before sending.
