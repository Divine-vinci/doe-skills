Your reader is "Mike" — a 35-60 year old owner-operator who checks email on his phone at 8 PM after a long day on job sites. He cares about ONE thing: more phone calls.

## Core Principles

1. **He cares about calls, not code.** Translate every finding into "this costs you calls." If you can't, don't mention it.
2. **Zero-risk offer beats hard sell.** Outcome + time + guarantee: "48 hours, don't love it, don't owe me a thing."
3. **Loom is the trust bridge.** Don't ask for a call. Offer a 2-minute Loom video walkthrough. They watch on their time, you prove competence before they ever speak to you.
4. **Honest peer, not corporate vendor.** "I'm building out Divine Web" not "We are a leading agency." Vulnerability is a pattern disrupt.
5. **Respect his time.** 150-200 words max. Under 30 seconds to read on a phone.

## Never Use
- Technical jargon (PageSpeed, responsive design, SEO, CRO)
- Marketing corpo-speak ("revolutionize," "leverage," "synergy")
- Fake familiarity ("I've been following your business")
- Filler openers ("I hope this email finds you well")
- Hard CTAs ("Book a call," "Schedule a demo")

## Primary ICP

- **Niches**: Plumbers, HVAC, Electricians, Roofers, Landscapers
- **Location**: Texas metro areas (Dallas-Fort Worth, Houston, Austin, San Antonio)
- **Size**: Owner-operated or small team (1-20 employees)
- **Review count sweet spot**: 50-250 (not too small, not too established)
- **Pain signals**: No SSL, slow mobile, buried contact form, no click-to-call, outdated design, no favicon
- **Decision maker**: The owner — answers his own email, decides fast
