# The Mike Profile — Home Service Owner-Operator

## The Industry at a Glance

- US plumbing market: $169.8 billion, 132,000+ businesses
- Over 50% of plumbing companies have NO dedicated digital marketing strategy
- Small plumbing companies (1-5 employees): $180K-$600K annual revenue
- Medium (6-20 employees): $600K-$1.8M annual revenue
- Average job ticket: $200-$800 (small repairs $150-$350, water heaters $850-$1,700)
- Net profit margins: 5-12% typical, 15-20% for well-run operations
- Gross margins: 45-65% (service-heavy push for 60-65%)
- Labor = 40-60% of costs, materials = 20-30%
- Marketing spend: $2,500-$12,000/month for those who invest

One plumbing company doubled revenue from $450K to $1.1M after professional web marketing — that's the result you're selling toward.

## The Person: "Mike"

### Demographics
- **Age**: 35-60
- **Gender**: predominantly male (85%+ in trades)
- **Education**: trade school or apprenticeship, NOT college-educated typically
- **Location**: suburban or semi-urban areas in metro regions
- **Income**: $75K-$200K depending on business size

### Daily Schedule
```
5:30-6:00 AM   — Wake up, coffee, check phone for overnight messages
6:00-6:30 AM   — Load truck with tools, fittings, materials
6:30-7:00 AM   — Drive to first job, maybe check email at a red light
7:00 AM-4:00 PM — ON JOB SITES all day. Hands dirty. No computer.
                  Takes calls between jobs. Checks phone at lunch.
4:00-5:00 PM   — Finish last job, snap photos, write job summary
5:00-6:00 PM   — ADMIN TIME: invoices, materials orders, customer follow-ups
6:00-8:00 PM   — Home with family. Phone email check at dinner.
8:00-9:00 PM   — THIS IS WHEN HE READS YOUR EMAIL. Tired, on phone, in bed.
```

**Key insight**: Mike reads email on his PHONE, at NIGHT, when he's tired. Your email must be short enough to read in 15 seconds on a 6-inch screen.

### How Mike Makes Decisions
- **Gut-driven, not data-driven.** He doesn't look at spreadsheets.
- **Trusts what he can SEE.** A demo site = proof he can see and click.
- **Decides fast.** No committee, no board meeting. Yes or no, usually same day.
- **Price-sensitive but not cheap.** He'll pay if the value is obvious.
- **Burned before by "marketing guys"** who promised results and delivered nothing.
- **Hates contracts and long-term commitments.** Prefers one-time or month-to-month.

### Technology Comfort
- Uses smartphone for everything (calls, texts, email, Facebook, Google Maps)
- May use ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, or Jobber for scheduling (or just a paper calendar)
- Has a Google Business Profile (someone set it up, he barely touches it)
- May run Google Ads (often set up by a third party, he just pays the bill)
- **Does NOT understand**: PageSpeed scores, responsive design, SEO, conversion rates
- **DOES understand**: "my phone isn't ringing enough" and "customers can't find me"

### What Mike Cares About (In Order)
1. **More phone calls** — every call is a potential $200-$800 job
2. **Looking legitimate** — his website reflects on his business reputation
3. **Not getting scammed** — he's been burned by marketing people before
4. **Keeping things simple** — he doesn't want to manage a website, he wants it to work
5. **Getting back to work** — he didn't start a plumbing business to do marketing

### What Mike Does NOT Care About
- PageSpeed scores (meaningless number to him)
- "Responsive design" (jargon — say "works on phones" instead)
- SEO rankings (say "shows up when people search for plumbers near me")
- Conversion rate optimization (say "turns more visitors into calls")
- Design trends (say "looks professional and modern")
- Analytics dashboards (say "you'll get more calls")

### What Makes Mike Reply

1. **You showed him something REAL** — a website with HIS business name on it
2. **You called out a problem he already suspected** — "9 seconds to load" confirms his gut
3. **The CTA is zero-risk** — "Take a look" not "Book a strategy call"
4. **It's SHORT** — one screen-scroll on mobile, max

### What Makes Mike DELETE

1. Generic pitch — "I help businesses improve their online presence"
2. Too long — more than one screen on mobile
3. Fake familiarity — "I've been following your business..." (he knows you haven't)
4. Hard sell — "Book a call" from a stranger
5. No proof — just words. The demo link IS the proof.
