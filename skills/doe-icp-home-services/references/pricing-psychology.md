# Pricing Psychology for Home Service ICP

Understanding how Mike thinks about money is the difference between closing at $500 and losing at $500.

## What Mike Compares You To

When Mike hears your price, he's mentally comparing it to:

| Option | Price Range | Mike's perception |
|--------|------------|------------------|
| His nephew who built the last site | $0-200 | "Free/cheap but it looked terrible" |
| Fiverr freelancer | $100-500 | "Cheap but probably junk" |
| Local web agency quote | $3,000-10,000 | "Too expensive, those guys are scammers" |
| His monthly Google Ads spend | $300-1,000/mo | "I pay this already and it kinda works" |
| Emergency plumbing tools | $500-2,000 | "I buy tools for this price all the time" |

**Key insight**: Mike doesn't compare you to other web designers. He compares you to his other business expenses. Frame your pricing in those terms.

---

## The Sweet Spot

### $500 One-Time — The No-Brainer Price

**Why it works**:
- Less than one month of wasted Google Ads
- Same ballpark as a decent set of plumbing tools
- Feels like "cheap enough to try without risk"
- Owner-operators can approve without checking with anyone

**Frame it as**:
- "Less than what you spend on Google Ads in a month"
- "One new customer pays for it twice"
- "No monthly fees — you pay once, you own it"

### $750-1,000 — Reasonable for Established Businesses

**When to use**:
- Business is doing $300K+/year (you can tell from review count + reviews mentioning years in business)
- They have 5+ employees (real business, not solo)
- They want additional features (booking form, photo gallery, blog)

**Frame it as**:
- "Still less than a week's revenue for your business"
- "Includes {specific upgrade} — that's usually an extra $X"

### $1,500 — Max for Small Owner-Operators

**When to use**:
- Full redesign including copywriting
- Multiple pages (home + services + about + contact + gallery)
- SEO optimization included
- 1 month of hosting bundled

**Never go above $1,500 for owner-operators.** They stop buying and start getting suspicious.

### Floor Price: $300

**Use only for**:
- First 2-3 clients (you need testimonials)
- Client who's on the fence but would convert at lower price
- Clients with smaller businesses where $500 is genuinely too much

**Never go below $300.** Below that:
- Signals desperation
- Attracts difficult clients
- Can't profitably deliver
- Damages your pricing power forever

## How to Frame the Price

### Anchoring Technique

```
"Most agencies charge $3,000-$10,000 for a site like this. 
I'm charging $500 because I've automated most of the work."
```

The $3K-$10K anchor makes $500 feel like a steal.

### Budget Shift Technique

```
"What are you spending on Google Ads right now? [Answer: $500/mo]
Okay — so one month of ads = this website, forever. 
Except this website never stops working."
```

Shifts the mental bucket from "marketing expense" to "infrastructure investment."

### ROI Framing

```
"Your average job is what, around $400? 
This website needs to bring you 1-2 extra customers to pay for itself. 
After that, it's all profit."
```

Makes the math obvious. Mike can do this math in his head instantly.

### Ownership Framing

```
"No monthly fees. No contracts. You pay once, you own the site. 
If you want me to keep maintaining it, that's separate — your choice."
```

Mike hates recurring costs and contracts. Ownership = freedom = trust.

## Payment Terms

### What Works

- **Half upfront, half on delivery**: $250 to start, $250 when the site is live. Reduces his risk, reduces yours.
- **Full upfront with guarantee**: "$500 now. If you don't love it in 7 days, full refund." Builds trust.
- **Stripe payment link**: One click, pay with card, done. No invoices, no forms.

### What Doesn't Work

- **Net 30/60**: Mike will forget and never pay
- **Multi-month contracts**: Instant rejection
- **Complex invoicing**: Anything requiring accounting software scares him
- **Monthly subscription for the website itself**: Only for hosting, not for the build

## The Upsell Ladder

After closing the initial website, these are your upsell opportunities, ordered by ease of close:

### Tier 1: Immediate upsells (offer at delivery)

1. **Monthly hosting + updates**: $50-100/mo
   - Frame: "I'll keep your site fast and fix anything that breaks"
   - Margin: ~80%

2. **Google Business Profile optimization**: $200 one-time
   - Frame: "I'll optimize your Google listing so you show up more in searches"
   - Takes 2-3 hours, one-time revenue

### Tier 2: 30 days after delivery

3. **Basic SEO setup**: $300-500 one-time
   - Frame: "Let's make sure Google can find you for 'plumber in Dallas' searches"
   - Add schema markup, optimize meta tags, submit sitemap

4. **Monthly SEO + reporting**: $200-500/mo
   - Frame: "I'll keep improving your rankings and send you a monthly report"
   - This becomes recurring revenue

### Tier 3: 60-90 days after delivery (via Amplify Voice stack)

5. **AI Receptionist**: $500 setup + $200/mo
   - Frame: "An AI that answers every call 24/7, books appointments, texts you the details"
   - Uses your existing Retell infrastructure

6. **Auto-Review System**: $300 setup + $100/mo
   - Frame: "Automatically texts customers after a job asking for a Google review"

7. **Social Media Content**: $300/mo for 4 videos
   - Frame: "I'll create 4 videos a month promoting your business on Facebook"
   - Uses your UGC pipeline

### The Math on Upsells

One website client can become:
- Month 1: $500 (website)
- Month 2-12: $200/mo hosting + SEO = $2,200
- Month 3-12: $200/mo AI receptionist = $2,000
- Month 6-12: $300/mo content = $2,100

**Total Year 1 value per client: ~$6,800**

That's the real business. The website is the wedge.

## Pricing Red Flags

### Signs he's about to lowball you

- "What's your best price?"
- "Can you do it for less?"
- "I saw someone on Fiverr for $50..."
- "My nephew said he could do it for free..."

**Response**: Hold firm on $500. If he really can't afford it:
> "I hear you. Tell you what — I can do a simpler version for $300. Fewer pages, less customization, but it'll still be faster and look better than what you have now."

Don't negotiate further. If he says no to $300, he's not your customer.

### Signs he's going to ghost

- Enthusiastic first reply, then silence
- "Let me check with my wife/partner"
- "I need to think about it over the weekend"

**Response**: One gentle follow-up 48 hours later. Then let it go.

### Signs he's ready to pay RIGHT NOW

- Asks about timeline ("how fast can you deliver?")
- Asks about payment ("how do I pay you?")
- Asks a specific question about the demo ("can we change the colors?")
- Replies quickly after seeing the demo
- Says "this is exactly what I need"

**Response**: Move FAST. Send payment link immediately. Don't overthink, don't add extras, don't try to upsell. Close the deal today.
