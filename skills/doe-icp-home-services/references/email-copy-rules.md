# Email Copy Rules for Home Service ICP

Exact rules for writing emails that Mike will actually open, read, and reply to.

## Subject Lines

### What Works for Mike

- `{Business Name} — quick question about your website`
- `{Business Name} — I noticed something on your site`
- `Built this for {Business Name} — take a look?`
- `{City} plumber? I have an idea for you`
- `Your {niche} website — 2 things I noticed`
- `{Business Name} — 9 seconds` (specific, mysterious, triggers curiosity)

### What DOESN'T Work

- "Revolutionize your online presence" (too corporate)
- "Free website audit" (screams spam)
- "Grow your plumbing business with AI" (alien language to Mike)
- "I help businesses like yours..." (generic, instant delete)
- Anything with ALL CAPS
- Anything with emojis 🚀 💰 ⚡ (triggers spam filters AND looks cheap)
- Anything with [brackets] or (parentheses)
- Numbered/templated: "5 ways to..." or "How to..."
- First name only: "Mike?" (feels like a scam)

### Subject Line Rules

1. **Under 6 words** — gets fully shown on mobile preview
2. **Lowercase preferred** — feels more personal, less marketing
3. **No punctuation tricks** — no !!!, no ???, no → arrows
4. **Reference HIM** — business name or city in the subject
5. **Question format or observation format** works best
6. **Never use "Re:" or "Fwd:"** — that's a scam tactic, kills trust instantly

## Opening Lines

### What Works

- "Your site loads in {X} seconds on mobile — most people leave after 3."
- "I noticed {business_name} doesn't have a click-to-call button on your site."
- "Saw your {rating}-star rating on Google — your website doesn't show any of those reviews."
- "I looked at your Google Ads — they're pointing to a page that takes {X} seconds to load."
- "Your Google listing is solid, but your actual website looks like it's from 2010."
- "Saw you're running ads for water heater installs — but the page they link to doesn't have a phone number."

### What DOESN'T Work

- "I hope this email finds you well" (instant delete — Mike has seen this 1000 times)
- "I'm reaching out because..." (nobody cares why you're reaching out)
- "I've been following your business..." (he knows you haven't)
- "As a fellow entrepreneur..." (cringe)
- "I noticed you could benefit from..." (condescending)
- "My name is Ayo and I..." (nobody cares who you are yet)
- "I came across your business..." (everyone says this)

### Opening Line Rules

1. **Lead with HIS world, not yours.** First word should be "Your" or about him.
2. **Be specific.** "9 seconds" beats "slow." "4.8 stars" beats "good reviews."
3. **Tie to impact immediately.** Don't just state a problem — hint at the cost.
4. **No throat-clearing.** Skip the intro. Jump straight to the observation.

## Call to Action (CTA)

### What Works

- "Worth a look?" (lowest friction possible)
- "Want me to send more details on pricing?"
- "If this looks interesting, just reply 'yes' and I'll share next steps"
- "Should I send you a 2-minute walkthrough?"
- "Want the link?" (for follow-up emails)
- "Make sense?" (conversational, low commitment)

### What DOESN'T Work

- "Book a 30-minute strategy call" (too much commitment for a cold email)
- "Schedule a demo" (the demo is already in the email, dumb to ask)
- "Let's set up a meeting" (he's on job sites, not in meetings)
- "Jump on a quick call?" (Mike hates phone meetings with strangers)
- "Click here to learn more" (salesy, links in CTAs look like spam)
- "Fill out this form" (zero chance)

### CTA Rules

1. **One CTA per email.** Not two options. Not three. One.
2. **Match commitment to relationship.** Cold = tiny ask. Warm = small ask. Hot = real ask.
3. **Make it a one-word reply.** "Yes," "interested," or even just "?" should be enough.
4. **Never use the word "call" in a cold email CTA.** Saves it for warm leads.
5. **Questions work better than commands.** "Worth a look?" > "Take a look."

## Body Copy Rules

### Structure That Works

```
[Observation about HIS business — 1 line]
[Why it matters to HIS phone ringing — 1 line]
[What I built for him + link — 1 line]
[Zero-friction CTA — 1 line]
[Signature]
```

### Length Rules

- **Cold email**: Under 75 words total
- **Follow-up #1**: Under 50 words
- **Follow-up #2 (breakup)**: Under 40 words

### Tone Rules

- Use contractions (I'm, don't, you're) — feels human
- Read it aloud — if it sounds like marketing, rewrite
- Second person dominant — "you" and "your" > "I" and "we"
- No adjectives unless they add info — "fast website" not "lightning-fast website"

### What to NEVER include

- Emojis
- Bullet points (feels like a presentation, not a message)
- Multiple paragraphs (one or two max)
- Your background/credentials
- Testimonials in the first email
- Images (triggers spam filters)
- Hyperlinks besides the one demo link
