# Email Copy Rules — Sav Zero-Risk Framework (v2)

Updated: April 16, 2026. Based on the Sav/Nate Herk cold email playbook + 4 verified DOE sends.

---

## The 5-Part Email Structure

### Part 1: Verified Compliment
Cite exact rating + review count. Verifiable in 5 seconds.
- "Saw [business] sitting at [rating] stars with [N] reviews in [city] — that's hard to earn."

### Part 2: Honest Positioning + Named Case Study
Pattern disrupt — sound like a peer, not an agency. Reference a real business.
- "I'm building out Divine Web — recently helped Same Day Plumbing in Irving tighten up their mobile site and conversion path."

### Part 3: One Verified Observation
ONE specific thing traceable to real data or manually confirmed on mobile. If no data backs it, use generic language.

### Part 4: Offer (Outcome + Time + Guarantee)
The Sav zero-risk formula: desired outcome + timeframe + risk reversal.
- "I can turn this into a clean, phone-first site that gets more visitors to call you — 48 hours, and if you don't love it, you don't owe me a thing."

### Part 5: Loom CTA + Signature
ONE ask only. A 2-minute Loom video walkthrough.
- "Mind if I walk you through a 2-minute Loom showing what I'd change?"
- Signature: — Ola Oluwaseun / Divine Web · divineweb.co

## Subject Lines — Cliffhanger / Internal Style

Sound like a colleague or someone who knows the business:

| Pattern | Example |
|---|---|
| Internal flag | `saw this on your site` |
| Colleague tone | `thought you should know` |
| Personal | `[first-name] - quick one` |
| Inquiry | `is [business-name] still taking on work?` |
| Thread reply | `re: [business-name]` |

**Avoid**: Title Case, "Free audit", "Website redesign", emojis, ALL CAPS, marketing language.

---

## Tone: Honest Peer

- Contractions and fragments OK
- "I'm building" not "We are a leading agency"
- Vulnerability is a pattern disrupt vs corporate cold email
- "You/your" dominates over "I/we"
- Read aloud — if it sounds like marketing, rewrite

---

## Forbidden

- Technical jargon (PageSpeed, responsive, SEO, CRO)
- Fabricated load times, UI bugs, percentages, dollar amounts
- Fake prototypes ("I sketched a version", "I built a mockup")
- Multiple CTAs
- Wrong signature (must be Ola Oluwaseun, Divine Web · divineweb.co)
- Emojis, bullet points in emails, images, multiple links

---

## Length

- **Cold email**: 150-200 words
- **Follow-up #1**: Under 80 words
- **Follow-up #2 (breakup)**: Under 50 words

Read aloud. If over 30 seconds on a phone, trim.
